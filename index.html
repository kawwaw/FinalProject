Visualising Education in Denmark

On this page, we follow a cohort of Danish population born in 1987 and their journey through the educational system between the years 2006 when they reached adulthood and 2017 when only about a fifth of the studied group were still in education.

This part illustrates how the cohort of young people born in 1987 over time graduate educations. The plot follows the same cohort over time. Chances in the total population number corresponds to either net immigration or net emmigration og the area selected.

The top level "not stated" is primarily immigrants, that has not graduated a Danish educational institution yet. When they do, they will be placed in the other categories.

There are big differences in the education and migration patterns between regions. Especially for the high educacional levels. Region Hovedstaden has a large population of people with masters degrees compared to any other region.

Description

This visualisation shows the relative number of Danish residents born in 1988 attending each of the educational levels recognised in Denmark, from Primary Education to PhD programmes, in a stacked area plot.

There is also a special category formed by the individuals who are no longer in education.

On the right-hand side, next to the chart, a number of selectors can be found. These can be used to interact with the chart and display more specific subsets of the data. First, the map can be used to select a particular region in Denmark. By clicking the avatars below, it is possible to show data for women or men, respectively. Finally, pressing the 'cross' icon resets the visualisation.

A few interesting observations can be made in this plot. As one could expect, the number of people in education drops significantly between 18 and 29 years of age. However, we see that the number drops sharply between the ages of 18 and 20 but then a large portion of the population gets to higher education.

We can clearly see that the portion of the population attending higher education is largest in the neighbourhood of Denmark's capital. On the other end of the rank, we would find the Zealand Region (Region Sjælland).
